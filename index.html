Reading Your Fence's Vital Signs

When posts begin tilting, the cause is almost always below ground. Frost-thaw cycles, shallow footings, or erosion can compromise stability.

Spaces between boards are typically due to wood shrinkage during the drying process—often seen with pressure-treated pine.

Sagging gates usually indicate issues with hinges or weakening frames, often accelerated by moisture.

Splintering and cracking indicate that wood is deteriorating—if left unchecked, cosmetic issues become structural failures.

Beyond Repair: When Prevention Becomes Your Best Strategy for your Fence

Schedule semi-annual inspections—ideally spring and fall
Keep soil and mulch from direct contact with fence materials
Trim vegetation that holds moisture against the fence
Clean wooden fences every 2-3 years and reseal as recommended
Tighten hardware annually before small wobbles become structural issues
Address drainage problems that direct water toward fence lines
