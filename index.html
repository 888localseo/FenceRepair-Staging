Reading Your Fence's Vital Signs

When fence repair posts begin tilting, it’s a sign of underground issues—frost-thaw cycles, shallow footings, or erosion.

Gaps between boards are typically due to wood shrinkage as lumber dries—common with pressure-treated pine.

Sagging gates indicate hinge or frame issues, often exacerbated by moisture exposure.

Splintering and cracking reveal deteriorating wood—if left unchecked, cosmetic issues can develop into structural failures.

Beyond Repair: When Prevention Becomes Your Best Strategy for Your Fence

Schedule semi-annual inspections—ideally spring and fall
Keep soil and mulch from direct contact with fence materials
Trim vegetation that holds moisture against the fence
Clean wooden fences every 2-3 years and reseal as recommended
Tighten hardware annually before small wobbles become structural issues
Address drainage problems directing water toward fence lines
